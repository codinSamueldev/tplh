How to handle online interviews?

Intro

Interviews are the most challenging situations we can face in our professional career, and knowing how to ace them can give us confidence, which leads landing that job you are applying for.

Here I'm sharing with you tips and how to answer most common questions interviewers ask, so you can exceed expectations.

Technical requirements

First thing first, it is necessary to be aware of our main tools in order to avoid wasting time and giving an astonishing first impression which stands you among other interviewees according to indeed. Be conscious of the following ones:

    • Your Internet connection must have at least 5mbps (Mega Bytes per Second).
      Laptop, Desktop PC or Smartphone (Your PC must have camera, if not get a webcam device).
      Find a quiet place with good lighting and no external noises.
      Test audio and video settings hours before the interview.
      Turn off phone and computer notifications, you don't want to know your favorite band are spliting up during an interview.
    • Common questions

    After checking your tools, let's take something into account. It is required to do a research about the company you are applying for since you want to know the requirements for the position, dress code, values and its culture. Then, the neat section of the interview which is, how to ace questions? I will introduce you to the common ones.

    Can you tell me about yourself?

    The interviewer is looking for professional background, strenghts related of the position, academic background, goals you want to achieve in the position and confidence. Let me share with you a script you can take advantage of (Remember to paraphrase it according to your position).

    I am a (Professional role) Some of my strengths are (Mention minimum three strengths that are aligned with the role) I studied/I'm studying (Specialized knowledge & education) I recently worked as a (position and company) Where I (List of duties, obligations or responsibilities) Now I am looking for (Goal).

    "I am a highly qualified professional with a Degree in Business Administration and over five years of experience in the areas of sales, management, accounting and marketing within different industries. Some strengths I have developed are my excellent communication skills, attention to detail, teamwork, problem solving skills and being innovative especially in sales strategies. Since I am completely bilingual, I am able to communicate effectively with people from different countries. Now I am looking for a new challenge as an international sales manager. I would like to contribute with my qualification and experience in order to reach company goals."

    Source: Platzi.

    Why should we hire you?

    Highlight what you like about the company and why you would be a good fit, select the accurate words to sound confident of your skill-set, nor egocentric, nor immature. Lastly, focus on the value you will bring.

    "I admire your company's results in the industry and I have experience in the services your company provides. I am goal-oriented and I deliver exceptional results, such as improving sales by 80%, and implementing a strategy to lower costs while maintaining quality. Since I have worked with people from different countries I adapt easily and work well with other people. I am sure I would be a good match for this position. I am interested in international sales and I am committed to creating innovative strategies to achieve company goals."
